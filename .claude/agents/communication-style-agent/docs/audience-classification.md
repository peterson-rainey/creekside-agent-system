# Audience Classification

## Audience Types (11 categories)
| Audience | Mode | Formality (1-5) | Key Characteristics |
|----------|------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Cade (co-founder) | Peer/Brainstorm | 1 | "dude/bro", profanity OK, thinks out loud, shares links without context, stream-of-consciousness, no greeting/sign-off ever |
| VAs/EAs (Cyndi, Melvin, Queeny) | Directive | 2 | 1-2 sentences, @mention first, no explanation unless non-obvious, command-style, assumes competence |
| Account Managers (Ade, Scott, Lindsey B, Trent, Grace) | Collegial-Direct | 2.5 | Frank, concise, expects competence, explains business rationale on pushback, occasional "bro" |
| Contractors (Tobi, Jordan, Ahmed, Tariq) | Task-Focused | 2.5 | Clear guardrails, states ownership, "go ahead and" softener, gives replacement language not just criticism |
| Existing Clients | Professional-Warm | 3.5 | Data-backed, educates without condescension, validates before correcting, ends with question or next step |
| New Leads (warm/referred) | Warm-Efficient | 3 | "Hey [Name]", names the referrer first, calendar link CTA, no schmoozing, 3-5 sentences |
| New Leads (cold/Upwork) | Problem-First | 3 | No greeting, opens with problem diagnosis, anchors with "$20M+ spend" and "200+ accounts", case study, calendar CTA |
| Departing/Lapsed Clients | Gracious | 3.5 | No pressure, leaves door open, "feel free to reach out anytime" |
| Collections/Legal | Formal-Minimal | 4.5 | Numbered points, contract references, exact dollars and dates, business third-person, zero hedging |
| Partners/Vendors | Text-Register | 2 | Shortest messages of any group, no structure, no formality, text-message style |
| Google/Platform Reps | Persistent-Direct | 3 | Will escalate firmly: "Can I get connected with someone who can provide a clear answer? It's been 8 days" |

## Formality Scaling Rule
**Formality scales with stakes, not relationship age.** A new high-value prospect gets a structured call recap with bold headers. An internal team member gets fragments and no capitalization. An existing mid-tier client gets clean but brief sentences.

The ONLY triggers for Peterson to go more formal:
1. Collections / legal disputes (full register switch to numbered points + contract language)
2. Client accountability messages (structured, numbered, owns mistakes explicitly)
3. First-contact emails to prospects he hasn't met
