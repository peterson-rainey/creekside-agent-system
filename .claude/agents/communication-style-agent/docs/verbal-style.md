# Verbal Style Appendix

> **DO NOT** apply these patterns to written messages. This section exists ONLY for:
> - Drafting call scripts or talking points
> - Preparing Peterson for verbal presentations
> - Understanding how Peterson communicates differently in speech vs writing

## Filler Word Signature (per 1,000 words)
| Word | Rate | Meaning |
|------|------|---------|
| "like" | 18.1 | Background rhythm -- constant |
| "just" | 13.0 | Extreme softener for instructions |
| "kind of" | 4.5 | Hedge/softener |
| "actually" | 2.3 | Introduces corrections or additions |
| "obviously" | 1.7 | Signals assumed shared knowledge |
| "essentially" | 1.4 | Always precedes the plain-English explanation |
| "basically" | 1.3 | Interchangeable with "essentially" |
| "honestly" | 0.5 | Incoming candor or self-disclosure |

## Call Openers by Meeting Type
**Internal (Cade, team):** Zero warm-up. Jumps in mid-thought.
**Client calls:** Brief geography small talk, then structure.
**Sales/Prospect calls:** Most structured. States agenda upfront.

## How He Explains Technical Concepts (verbal)
1. "So essentially..." -- leads the plain-English translation
2. "It's kind of like..." -- uses analogy
3. "The reason we do [X] is..." -- explains WHY first, then WHAT
4. Admits uncertainty mid-explanation without embarrassment
5. Self-deprecating humor: "I sound like an insane person but..."

## Sales Objection Handling (verbal)
- Hard commitment filter: "If you're not willing to commit to three months, we're not going to be a good fit for you"
- Direct qualifying: "What would cause you to essentially say, hey, you guys are obviously the perfect partners for us, let's sign up today"
- Honest expectation: "I don't want to lie to you and basically say they'll immediately get up and running"
