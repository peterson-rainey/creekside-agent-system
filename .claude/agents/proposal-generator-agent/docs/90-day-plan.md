# 90-Day Client Plan Framework

Delivered to new clients as part of onboarding. Referenced in proposals to show what working with Creekside looks like. Peterson's philosophy: "We're not just taking over ads -- we're establishing a system."

This framing sets expectations, reduces churn risk in the first 90 days, and differentiates from agencies that promise results in Week 1.

## The Three-Month Framework

### Month 1 -- Foundation and Learning
- Account audit and restructure (if taking over existing campaigns)
- Pixel / conversion tracking setup and verification
- Campaign creation: 1-2 core campaigns per platform
- Baseline performance benchmarks established
- Onboarding call to review strategy and confirm goals
- Weekly optimization begins

**Customize for each client by naming their specific starting state.** Examples:
- "Restructure your existing Google account, which currently has 14 ad groups with no negative keywords and a bidding strategy misaligned with your lead generation goals"
- "Build your first Google Search campaign from scratch, starting with your highest-revenue service line"
- "Audit and reset your Meta campaigns, which have been running the same creative for 6+ months"

### Month 2 -- Optimization
- A/B testing ad copy and creative
- Keyword expansion or pruning based on data
- Audience refinement
- Budget reallocation based on early performance signals
- Mid-point strategy call
- Landing page recommendations delivered

**Customize by referencing their stated goals.** If they mentioned a specific CPA target or lead volume goal, name it: "We'll be optimizing toward your target of under $50 cost per lead."

### Month 3 -- Performance and Scale
- Campaign scaling on winning ad sets / keywords
- Lookalike audience building (Meta)
- Remarketing campaigns launched
- Performance vs. baseline comparison
- 90-day review call with recommendations for Month 4+

**Customize by tying to their business timeline.** If they mentioned a seasonal push or revenue target: "We'll be positioned to scale aggressively into your Q3 busy season."

## Onboarding Task List (ClickUp template -- what actually happens at kickoff)

1. Run audit of existing ads (if applicable)
2. Schedule review call before launching new ads
3. Pixel setup assistance (Jordan Tryon is the contact for technical setup)
4. Facebook page access request
5. Website access request
6. Contract signed (CRITICAL -- do not launch without signed contract)
7. Payment method confirmed

**Do not include the ClickUp internal process in the proposal.** The proposal shows the client-facing 90-day plan only. The internal task list is for the team.

## Client Communication Cadence (include in proposals as a feature)

- **Weekly:** Brief performance update via Loom or Google Chat message
- **Bi-weekly:** Comprehensive written report with data analysis and recommendations
- **Monthly:** Strategy call (scheduled in calendar)
- **Ad hoc:** Immediate response to client concerns (Peterson's standard: within the hour)

## How to Tailor the 90-Day Plan in a Proposal

1. Read the discovery call transcript (Step 2 of the main workflow)
2. Identify: current state (existing account vs. brand new), stated goal, specific pain points
3. Customize Month 1 to name their current state specifically
4. Tie Month 2 optimization language to their stated metric goals
5. Tie Month 3 scale language to their growth timeline or seasonal business needs

Avoid generic language like "we'll optimize your campaigns." Every bullet should be specific enough that the client recognizes their own situation.
