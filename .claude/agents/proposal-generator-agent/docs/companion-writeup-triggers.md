# Companion Writeup Triggers

## What Is a Companion Writeup

A companion writeup is a standalone branded .docx document that covers one topic in
depth. It is written to be self-contained -- the reader does not need to have been
on the discovery call. The typical use case: a prospect wants to share something with
a business partner, brother, spouse, or internal team who wasn't on the call.

Companion writeups are generated by `build_companion_writeup_docx.py` and attached
alongside the main proposal in the Gmail draft.

---

## Trigger Phrase Detection

After pulling and reading the Fathom transcript in Step 2, scan for any of these
trigger phrases. Detection is case-insensitive. Partial matches count.

### Group A -- Share with someone else (highest confidence)
- "share with my brother"
- "share with my partner"
- "share with my wife" / "share with my husband"
- "share with my team"
- "share with my manager"
- "my [family member / partner / colleague] would want to see"
- "can I show this to"
- "I want to run this by"

### Group B -- "Send me information on X" (medium confidence)
- "send me information on [X]"
- "send me a writeup on [X]"
- "can you put together something on [X]"
- "more detail on [X]"
- "explain how [X] works"
- "what does [X] look like"
- "I'd like to understand [X] better"
- "walk me through [X]"

### Group C -- Topic surfaced organically (lower confidence, use judgment)
- Prospect asked a deep technical question mid-call that Peterson answered verbally but
  the prospect said "got it, makes sense" without fully engaging
- Peterson said "I can send you more on that" or "I'll put something together"
- The topic was raised and tabled ("we can cover that later")

**For Group C:** Only flag a companion writeup if Peterson's reply on the call included
a commitment to follow up in writing. Do not generate speculatively.

## Valid Topics for Companion Writeups

These are topics Creekside can speak to authoritatively in a standalone document.
Generate automatically when a trigger fires for any of these:

| Topic | Suggested title |
|---|---|
| Conversion tracking (general) | "Conversion Tracking Setup" |
| Conversion tracking (phone calls / CallRail) | "Call Tracking Setup" |
| Attribution methodology / GCLID / UTM flow | "Attribution: How We Track Revenue to Ads" |
| Account audit findings (existing account) | "Account Audit Findings" |
| 90-day plan in detail | "90-Day Strategic Plan" |
| Why conversion tracking is non-optional | "Why Conversion Tracking Matters" |
| Google Ads vs Meta Ads (how they differ) | "Google Ads vs Meta Ads: What's Right for [Business]" |
| How Creekside's management process works | "How We Work: Creekside Management Process" |
| Reporting cadence and what they'll see | "What Your Reporting Looks Like" |

**Escalate to Peterson (do NOT generate automatically):**
- Competitor comparisons or "why not [other agency]" framing
- ROI projections or promised outcome ranges
- Contract or legal term explanations
- Anything touching a client we currently manage (conflict risk)
- Custom technical topic not on the list above

## Canonical Example: Shin Nagpal / Village Repair -- Conversion Tracking

**Discovery call date:** May 15, 2026

**Trigger that fired:** "can you send me information on conversion setup, exactly like
what that would look like... I'd like to share with my brother"

**Trigger group:** A (share with someone else) + B (send me information on X). Both
groups fired simultaneously on the same topic.

**Topic generated:** "Conversion Tracking Setup"
**Subtitle:** "What We Build for Village Repair"

**Content structure used:**
1. Why This Matters (the business case -- Google optimizes for calls, not revenue)
2. What Gets Built (CallRail, shop management API integration, GCLID/UTM, server-side)
3. What You'll See on Your End (questions they'll be able to answer)
4. Timeline (Week 1, 2, ongoing)
5. Why It's Not Optional (the three reasons)
6. Questions? (closing)

**Notable details:**
- Shin mentioned his shop management system has an API he writes against himself.
  The writeup referenced that directly ("you mentioned on the call that you write your
  own reports against the shop management API").
- The "Why It's Not Optional" section was explicit because Shin had signaled earlier
  in the call that he was budget-sensitive. Pre-empting the "can we skip this" question
  was deliberate.
- No Google Chat or Slack references anywhere (sales-stage rule: prospect-facing docs
  do not name internal tools).

**What the email body said:**
> "Two documents attached -- the proposal we discussed and a standalone conversion
> tracking writeup written so your brother can review it without needing background
> on the call."

## Email Handling When a Companion Is Generated

When Step 5.5 flags a companion writeup and Step 6 builds both files, update Steps 7
and 8 as follows:

**Gmail draft body changes:**
- Mention "two documents attached" in the first paragraph
- One sentence on what the writeup covers and who it's for
- Example: "I've also put together a standalone conversion tracking writeup your
  brother can read without any context from our call."

**`create_gmail_draft.py` call:**
- Add the companion .docx to the `attachments` list alongside the main proposal .docx

**Subject line:** Keep the standard subject. Do not add "+" or "and writeup" to the
subject -- it adds friction and is redundant given the body.

**Validator:** Run `validate_output.py` on the companion writeup file in addition to
the main proposal before proceeding to the Gmail draft step.
