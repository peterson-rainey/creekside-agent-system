# Proposal Types and Output Structure

## Three Proposal Types

### 1. Google Ads + Meta Ads Retainer Proposal (most common)

Scope: Ongoing Google Ads and/or Meta Ads management.

Sections (in order):
1. Executive Summary / Overview
2. Current State (what we learned from the call / audit)
3. Recommended Services (tailored to their business)
4. Ongoing Management (from template -- keep as-is, add lead-specific customization)
5. Platform Coverage (from template -- keep as-is)
6. Investment Structure (pricing plan with math and recommendation)
7. 90-Day Plan (customized per `docs/90-day-plan.md`)
8. Onboarding Timeline
9. Next Steps (specific, who does what by when)

Tone: Confident partnership. "Here's what we'd do together and why it works."

Never generic. For warm leads (discovery call done): reference specific things they said. Use their business name frequently.

### 2. Google Ads Audit

Scope: Full account audit before onboarding or as a standalone engagement.

Sections (in order):
1. Executive Summary
2. Account Health Overview
3. Campaign Structure
4. Keyword Strategy
5. Ad Copy
6. Conversion Tracking
7. Bidding and Budget
8. Quick Wins (3-5 items, fixable immediately)
9. 90-Day Roadmap
10. Pricing / Next Steps

Tone: Expert, consultative, finds real issues -- not generic. Reference live account data when available.

Cost: Standalone audit sometimes offered free as a trust-builder (live audit on discovery call). Formal written audit for existing prospects: pricing per engagement.

### 3. Project Scope

Scope: One-time or defined-duration engagement (e.g., account setup, audit + strategy, 90-day campaign build).

Sections:
1. Project Overview
2. Deliverables (specific, numbered)
3. Timeline (with milestones)
4. Investment
5. Terms
6. Next Steps

Tone: Specific, milestone-focused. This is a contract, not a pitch.

## Mandatory Sections for All Proposal Types

These sections must appear in every proposal regardless of type:
- Current state analysis (what we found -- specific, not vague)
- Recommended services (tailored to their business)
- Investment structure (always % of ad spend per the pricing model, except project scope which can be flat)
- Timeline
- Next steps (specific, with who does what by when)

## Audience Calibration (applies across all types)

| Lead Type | Opener | Credentials | Case Study |
|---|---|---|---|
| Existing client | Skip intro, lead with their data | Skip entirely | Only if introducing new service |
| Warm lead (call done) | Reference their pain point from call | Brief mention, 1 sentence | Lead with closest industry match |
| Cold prospect | Open with a problem they have | 1 short paragraph max | Lead with it -- put best result in paragraph 1 |

## Template Sections to Keep Verbatim

These sections from the live template should NOT be customized (keep as written):
- Ongoing Management and Optimization (Google Ads + Meta Ads activity lists)
- Platform Coverage (the placements breakdown)
- Payment and Contract Terms

Customize: Overview, Why Choose Creekside (add one lead-specific bullet if warranted), Onboarding Services, 90-Day Plan, Investment, Next Steps.
