# Communication Style for Proposals

Peterson's voice applied to long-form proposal documents.

## Absolute Rules (Apply to Every Proposal and Email)

- **NO em dashes.** Never. Replace with a colon, period, or restructure the sentence.
- **No filler words:** "world-class", "industry-leading", "synergy", "holistic", "leverage", "seamless", "bespoke"
- **Contractions always:** "we're", "you'll", "don't", "we've" -- never expanded form
- **Short paragraphs.** Bullets over prose for lists.
- **Specific over vague:** "$9.58 CPA" not "significantly reduced cost per acquisition"
- **No generic openers:** Never "Thank you for your interest" or "I hope this email finds you well"
- **Make it about them, not Creekside.** Every section should connect back to their goals.

## By Audience Type

### Warm Lead (discovery call completed)
- Reference their specific words: "You mentioned [X] -- here's how we address that..."
- Use their business name in headers, not "the client"
- Skip the generic agency intro -- they know who Creekside is
- Lead with their pain point, then the solution, then results proof
- Echo specific things they said on the call (pull from Fathom transcript)

### Cold Prospect (Upwork or outreach)
- Open with a problem they have, not with Creekside's credentials
- Case study leads -- put the most relevant result in the opening paragraph
- Keep proposals concise: problem, fit, proof, pricing, next step
- Avoid generic openers

### Existing Client (expansion or upsell)
- Skip all introduction and credentials entirely
- Lead with performance data from their own account
- Frame the upsell as a logical next step based on results: "Given your [X result], here's what we'd do next..."

## Tone by Proposal Type

- **Audit:** Technical authority. "Here's what we found. Here's why it matters. Here's how to fix it."
- **Retainer proposal:** Confident partnership. "Here's what we'd do together and why it works."
- **Project scope:** Business-like and specific. "Here are the deliverables, timeline, and price."

## What NOT to Do

- Don't use "synergy", "holistic", "leverage", "seamless", "bespoke"
- Don't promise specific ROI or guarantee results
- Don't make the proposal about Creekside -- make it about the prospect's goals
- Don't pad with agency history unless it's directly relevant to their niche
- Don't present pricing in a way that invites negotiation -- state it clearly and move to next steps
- Don't write more than 2 sentences before getting to the point

## Email Draft Rules (in addition to the above)

- Under 150 words
- Sign as "Peterson" only (not "The Creekside Marketing Team" or "Best, Peterson Rainey")
- No em dashes
- First sentence must be specific to their call (not generic)
- Subject line format: "[Business Name] -- Creekside Proposal + Next Steps"

## Peterson's Sales Philosophy (context -- don't put these in proposals verbatim)

- "You aren't selling them on a service, you are selling them on you"
- "People hate bullshit, be honest about your shortcomings"
- "If they mention a service you don't do, say you explicitly don't do it because jack of all trades are masters of none"
- "Avoid negotiating by saying you compete on quality, not price"

These inform the tone but are not to be quoted in proposals.
