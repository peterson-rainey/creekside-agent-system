# Audit Methodology

Source: Victory Land Sales audit session, March 2026, and ongoing Creekside audit practice.

## Google Ads Audit -- 9 Key Areas

Evaluate each area. For each: state what was found, why it matters, and the recommended fix.

1. **Bidding Strategy** -- Is it aligned with goals? (Maximize Conversions vs Target CPA vs Target ROAS)
2. **Keyword Strategy** -- Are high-performing match types enabled? (phrase vs broad vs exact; are negatives in place?)
3. **Campaign Structure** -- Are ad groups targeted effectively? Separate campaigns by location, service, audience?
4. **Ad Copy** -- Is Dynamic Keyword Insertion used? Dynamic Location Insertion? Strong CTAs present?
5. **Ad Extensions/Assets** -- Sitelinks (custom vs auto), structured snippets, callouts, lead forms present?
6. **Conversion Tracking** -- Are custom conversions set up and receiving data? CRM integration verified?
7. **Landing Pages** -- Trust factors present? (BBB, testimonials, reviews, clear CTA)
8. **Audience Signals** -- Proper audience lists? Exclusion lists (competitors, employees)?
9. **Geographic Targeting** -- City/zip targeting vs radius only? Correct coverage?

## Meta Ads Audit -- Key Areas

1. **Account Health** -- Domain verification, pixel events firing correctly, Events Manager clean
2. **Campaign Objectives** -- Aligned with business goal (leads vs purchase vs awareness)
3. **Audience Overlap** -- Preventing internal competition between ad sets
4. **Creative Performance** -- Video vs static, frequency tracking, creative fatigue signals
5. **Catalog Setup** (for e-commerce) -- Product feed quality, collection groupings
6. **Conversion Tracking** -- Meta pixel vs CAPI server-side, Stape integration
7. **Attribution Windows** -- Click + view attribution model configured correctly

## Audit Report Format (use this structure in proposals)

```
Account Overview
- Total spend [period]
- Active campaigns: [N]
- Enabled vs paused status

Performance Snapshot
- Current CPA/ROAS vs benchmark or stated goal
- [Always cite source: [source: table, id] or [from: live account review on [date]]]

Issues Found (prioritized by impact)
Critical:
1. [Issue] -- [Why it matters] -- [Fix]

Moderate:
2. [Issue] -- [Why it matters] -- [Fix]

Minor:
3. [Issue] -- [Why it matters] -- [Fix]

Quick Wins (3-5 items, high impact, fixable immediately)
- [Item]

Strategic Recommendations (structural changes, 30/60/90-day roadmap)
- [Item]

Next Steps (what Creekside does vs what client provides)
- Creekside: [actions]
- Client to provide: [access, assets, info]
```

## Audit Pricing

- Live audit on discovery call: FREE (trust-builder, standard part of sales process)
- Formal written standalone audit: pricing discussed per engagement
- Audit as part of onboarding (when retainer is signed): counted as onboarding fee ($1,000/platform) -- no separate audit charge

## Tone for Audit Sections

"Technical authority. Here's what we found. Here's why it matters. Here's how to fix it."

Be specific. Vague findings ("your ads could be better") destroy credibility. Specific findings ("your search campaigns have no negative keywords, which means roughly 20-30% of your spend is going to irrelevant queries") build trust.

Never fabricate findings. If the audit was done live on the call, reference that: "[from: live account review, [date]]". If no audit was done, do not invent issues.
