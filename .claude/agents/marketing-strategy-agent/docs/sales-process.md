# Sales Process & Differentiation

## 11-Step Discovery Framework
1. Rapid response — call within the first hour of inquiry
2. Pre-call warm-up — send intro video and 4 qualifying questions
3. Opening — "Tell me about your business and what you're looking for"
4. 5-minute qualifier — ad spend, urgency, decision makers, timeline
5. Discovery questions — budget, history, failures, goals, capacity
6. Deep-dive — expand pain points, get consequences, get vision of success
7. Live account audit — spend 10 minutes in their account, find issues, give free feedback; makes them trust you before you pitch
8. Present strategy — Canva deck with relevant case studies, PROACTIVELY in first 15 minutes (don't wait to be asked)
9. Pricing — wait for them to ask before presenting; present tiered structure
10. Close — trial close, present pricing, ask "what would it take to move forward this week?" then SILENCE
11. Follow-up cadence — Day 0: recap email; Day 3: relevant case study; Day 7: direct ask; Day 14: final nudge; monthly nurture after that

## Qualification Criteria
YES: $3K+/month per platform budget, scaling mindset, treats ads as investment, has a proven offer
NO: below $3K/month, status quo mindset, price shopping, wants to build their own agency, demands guarantees without context, blames every previous agency, won't give account access, micromanagers

## Peterson's Sales Philosophy (Verbatim)
- "You aren't selling them on a service, you are selling them on you"
- "People hate bullshit, be honest about your shortcomings"
- "If they mention a service you don't do, say you explicitly don't do it because jack of all trades are masters of none"
- "Avoid negotiating by saying you compete on quality, not price"

## How Peterson Differentiates on Calls
- On value-based pricing: "We're not charging based on the number of hours being worked on the account. We're charging based on the value that we're providing to the business owner."
- On profitability vs scalability: "There's a difference between profitability and scalability. A lot of people think, oh, let's just drive the cost per conversion down as low as possible. And then we'll just scale to infinity. That's not how that works."
- On defending % of ad spend: "The people that we're going to work with, they're not going to continue to work with us if we aren't driving revenue for their business and their business isn't profitable."
- On why not profit sharing: "We can't control the amount of profit they make. The offer would be too complex and too custom to every single business; that just doesn't make sense to scale out."
- On losing deals: The primary issue is trust/differentiation — "we didn't differentiate ourselves enough from the competition they were also talking to." Price shoppers aren't good clients and Creekside doesn't want them.
