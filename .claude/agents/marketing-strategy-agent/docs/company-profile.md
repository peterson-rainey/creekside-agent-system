## Positioning
- Tagline: "Senior Google and Meta ads management for US service and SaaS brands spending $10k-$100k per month. Improve ROAS in 90 days or we work free. Launch in 7 days."
- Category: Performance Marketing, PPC Agency, Paid Social, Media Buying, Growth Marketing
- Key differentiators: Live audits on first call, direct access to ad managers, month-to-month contracts, optimize for new customer growth (not just CPA)
- Services NOT offered: Organic SEO, content marketing, organic social, email marketing, brand design/web dev, direct mail. Peterson's own words: "jack of all trades are masters of none."

## Dual Positioning Strategy
- Generalist "platform specialist" positioning is intentionally correct on Upwork — there isn't enough niche demand from any single vertical on Upwork to specialize there. You can't be niche on Upwork. The platform specialist angle (Google Ads + Meta Ads only) IS the differentiation on Upwork.
- For paid ads and outbound targeting dental practices, the positioning shifts to cosmetic dental case generation specifically.
- This dual positioning (generalist on Upwork, niche for outbound/paid ads) is a viable TRANSITION strategy — Upwork provides stability while dental is being proven. The destination is niche dominance in dental, but Upwork generalism funds the transition.

## Common Objections & Responses
- On value-based pricing: "We're not charging based on the number of hours being worked on the account. We're charging based on the value that we're providing to the business owner."
- On defending % of ad spend: "The people that we're going to work with, they're not going to continue to work with us if we aren't driving revenue for their business and their business isn't profitable."
- On why not profit sharing: "We can't control the amount of profit they make. The offer would be too complex and too custom to every single business; that just doesn't make sense to scale out."
