## 3. THE AUDIT (15-20 minutes)

This is free-form based on what you see in the account. Below is a checklist of things to look for, but go with your experience and what jumps out first.

### Campaign Structure Check
- How many campaigns are running? Are they organized or a mess?
- Are they using CBO or ABO? Is the budget allocation making sense?
- Is there a clear funnel (prospecting vs retargeting)?
- Are there dead campaigns still spending?

**What to say when you find something:**
> "See this right here? You've got [X campaigns] running and [Y] of them are spending budget but not generating any consultations. That's $[amount] a month going to campaigns that aren't producing."

### Audience Targeting Check
- Are audiences too broad or too narrow?
- Is there audience overlap between ad sets?
- Are they using lookalikes? Custom audiences? Retargeting?
- Is the targeting actually reaching practice decision-makers or random people?

**What to say:**
> "Your targeting here is [too broad/overlapping/missing retargeting]. What that means is [explain the impact]. Here's what I'd do instead."

### Ad Creative Check
- Are they testing multiple creatives or running the same ad forever?
- Is the copy speaking to cosmetic/implant patients or generic?
- Are the images/videos professional or low quality?
- What's the click-through rate? Anything under 1% is a problem.

**What to say:**
> "Your best-performing ad has a [X]% click-through rate. The benchmark for dental is [Y]%. That tells me [diagnosis]."

### Landing Page Check
- Where are the ads sending traffic? Their website? A dedicated landing page?
- Does the landing page have a clear CTA?
- Is it mobile-optimized?
- Is there a disconnect between the ad promise and the landing page?

**What to say:**
> "You're sending all this traffic to [their website homepage / a weak page]. That's like paying for a billboard and then having a confusing storefront. The fix is [specific recommendation]."

### Conversion Tracking Check
- Is the Meta pixel installed and firing correctly?
- Are they tracking actual conversions (form fills, calls) or just page views?
- Is there any offline conversion tracking?

**What to say:**
> "Your tracking is [missing/broken/incomplete]. That means [Meta/Google] can't optimize for the right outcome. You're essentially telling the algorithm to find you more [page views] when you actually want [consultations]."

### Cost Per Consultation Math
- Calculate their actual cost per consultation from the data
- Compare to benchmarks ($800-$1,200 optimized, $1,500-$2,500 average)
- Show them the gap in dollars

**What to say:**
> "Based on what I'm seeing, your cost per consultation is about $[X]. The benchmark for an optimized cosmetic practice is $800 to $1,200. That gap is costing you $[monthly amount] in wasted spend."

### If They Aren't Running Ads
- Ask about their practice: services, revenue, patient volume, biggest challenge
- Walk them through what a campaign structure would look like for their practice
- Reference Dr. Laleh or Tooth Co numbers at their spend level
- Explain which platforms they should be on and why

## 4. DIAGNOSIS SUMMARY (2-3 minutes)

After walking through the account, summarize the top 3 findings.

"Okay, so here's what I'm seeing. You've got three main issues."

"First, [biggest issue -- e.g., 'your campaigns are optimizing for leads instead of consultations, which is why your cost per consultation is $2,200 instead of $1,000']."

"Second, [second issue -- e.g., 'you have no retargeting, which means everyone who visits your site and doesn't convert is gone forever']."

"Third, [third issue -- e.g., 'your tracking is broken, so Meta can't even see which ads are generating real patients']."

**Always end with a specific number:**

"If we just fixed [the biggest issue], based on your current spend of $[X] a month, that would save you roughly $[amount] a month and add approximately [Y] more consultations. Over a year, that's $[annual amount] in additional revenue."

## 5. THE TRANSITION (2-3 minutes)

**Only if they're engaged and asking questions. Do not force this.**

"So that's the audit. Those are real issues that are costing you money right now. Do you have any questions about what I showed you?"

**[Answer their questions]**

**If they ask how to fix it or who can help:**

"We can fix all of this. That's literally what we do. We specialize in paid ads for cosmetic and implant dental practices. Google, Meta, TikTok, programmatic, ChatGPT Ads, Bing. Nothing else."

**If they ask about pricing:**

"Our management fee is a percentage of your ad spend. It starts at 20% and comes down as your spend grows. At higher spend levels, the rate drops to 15%, then 10%.

At your spend level of [their reported spend], your management fee would be about [calculate]. That covers everything: ad management, landing page builds, creative production, reporting, CRM consultation. One fee, full service.

90-day initial term, then month-to-month after that. If we don't hit 3X return on your ad spend within 90 days, we work for free until we do. Multi-touch attribution tied to actual revenue."

**If they ask about exclusivity:**

"We won't work with a competing practice in your area. If we take you on, your market is yours."

**If they want to move forward:**

"Here's what happens next. I send you a simple agreement today. You sign it, and we start the onboarding process this week. We'll schedule a kickoff call where we get access to your ad accounts, review your current setup, and map out the creative and landing page strategy. First campaigns go live within 30 days.

Sound good?"

**(Then SILENCE. Wait for their answer.)**

**If they need to think about it:**

"Totally understand. I'll send you a follow-up email today with a summary of what we found in the audit and the specific recommendations. Take a look, talk to your partner if you need to, and let's reconnect on [specific day, 3-4 days out]. Does [day] at [time] work?"

**(Book the follow-up before hanging up.)**
