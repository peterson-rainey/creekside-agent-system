## 1. MY DENTAL AGENCY (mydentalagency.com)

**Founded:** 2015 | **HQ:** New Port Richey, FL | **Team:** 18 | **Pricing:** Custom (unpublished)

### STRENGTHS (Internal Positive)
- **Strong founder brand credibility.** Jackie Ulasewich Cullen has 10+ years dental lab marketing experience, published author ("Elite Dentistry"), contributor to Entrepreneur and DentistryIQ, featured on ADA panels. This gives authentic dental industry credibility that generalist marketers cannot match.
- **Clinical Advisory Board.** Three practicing dentists (Lee Brown DDS, David Pearce DDS, Karinna) advise the agency -- a genuine differentiator that signals clinical understanding.
- **Multi-channel content engine.** Podcast ("The My Dental Agency Podcast"), MDA LIVE streaming, blog, book as lead magnet, and Jackie's personal speaking brand create multiple touchpoints.
- **Anti-corporate positioning resonates.** Their "Distinctly Different" positioning against "mass marketing groups" appeals to independent dentists who fear being treated like a number. This is emotionally effective even if the proof is thin.
- **100% recommendation rate on Facebook** (12 reviews, 5.0 stars). While the sample is small, a perfect score is valuable.
- **Google Partner and Meta certified.** Table stakes, but confirmed.

### WEAKNESSES (Internal Negative)
- **Zero quantified case studies.** This is the most significant weakness. Testimonials are generic ("delivered on results") with no patient counts, revenue numbers, ROI figures, or CPL data. In a market where Delmain publishes 14+ detailed case studies, this is a critical credibility gap.
- **No published pricing.** Creates friction in the buying process. Prospects who comparison-shop will find Delmain, Practice Cafe, and Lasso MD prices easily while MDA requires a call.
- **No performance guarantees.** No money-back offer, no ROI commitment, no minimum patient count promise.
- **Minimal third-party review presence.** Only 12 Facebook reviews. No Clutch, G2, Trustpilot, or BBB profiles found. This looks thin next to Pain-Free Dental Marketing's 108 Google reviews or Delmain's 74 FeaturedCustomers references.
- **Small team (18) with broad service menu.** Trying to do everything (SEO, PPC, social, web design, video, branding) with 18 people means each function is thinly staffed.
- **Podcast is infrequent.** Only 14 episodes, bimonthly cadence. Compare to Gary Bird's two active podcasts or Chris Pistorius's weekly show.
- **No proprietary technology.** No PatientLoop, PatientPulse, or equivalent platform. Standard WordPress + Google/Meta stack.

### OPPORTUNITIES (External Positive)
- **Publish case study data to leapfrog competitors.** Most competitors also have weak case studies. Even 3-4 strong quantified examples would differentiate.
- **Leverage the Clinical Advisory Board more visibly.** This is unique -- turn it into content (interviews, joint webinars, "ask the dentist" series).
- **DSO market entry.** Their anti-corporate positioning could be reframed for emerging DSOs that want to retain independent brand identity.
- **HIPAA-compliant tracking expertise.** With Meta's 2025-2026 healthcare restrictions, agencies that can navigate pixel tracking, server-side implementation, and CAPI integration will command premium pricing.

### THREATS (External Negative)
- **Larger competitors with stronger proof.** Delmain (40-75 employees, 14+ case studies, Google Premier Partner) and Wonderist (57 employees, INC 5000 six years running) can outspend and out-credential MDA.
- **AI-powered competitors.** Cosmetics Growth and KickStart Dental Marketing are already using AI for lead response and call tracking. MDA shows no AI capability.
- **Meta healthcare ad restrictions (2026 wave).** The expected Q2 2026 restrictions on conversion events and data-sharing will hit agencies that lack server-side tracking expertise.
- **DSO consolidation.** As 39% of dental offices become DSO-affiliated by 2026, agencies serving only independent practices face a shrinking addressable market.
- **Rising patient acquisition costs.** Average dental patient acquisition now $312 per lead, up significantly. Agencies that cannot demonstrate cost efficiency will lose clients.

- **Strongest case study numbers in the UK market.** London Invisalign practice: 12 to 80 new patients/month, revenue GBP 1.4M to GBP 4.2M. Manchester implant specialist: 9x patient growth, 5:1 ROI. These are genuinely impressive, specific numbers.

- **AI-powered patient engagement tools.** They reference AI-driven tools and DenGro integration, suggesting technical sophistication beyond basic agency work.
- **Aggressive SEO comparison strategy.** Comparison pages ("DominateDental vs Wonderist," "vs WeKnow," "vs Dental Focus") capture competitors' search traffic.
- **5.0 Google rating (18 reviews).** Clean reputation.
- **Dan Ashburn hints at exclusivity.** LinkedIn tagline suggests "100% exclusive" positioning.

### WEAKNESSES (Internal Negative)
- **UK-only market.** Not a direct US competitor, which limits relevance but also means no US case studies, no US regulatory knowledge (HIPAA, state advertising rules).
- **Very small team (7-10).** Severe capacity constraints for serving multi-location practices.
- **No podcast or YouTube presence.** Compared to US competitors with active content engines, DD has no thought leadership platform.
- **Infusionsoft/Keap dependency.** An aging marketing automation platform that's been losing market share to GoHighLevel, HubSpot, and ActiveCampaign.

- **Dan Wang's technical background.** CS degree from UW, former software programmer in healthcare tools. More technically credible than most agency founders.
- **Strong case study numbers.** Fayetteville Dental Studio: 3.2X revenue growth, 47 new patient bookings/month in 90 days. Innovative Dental Implant Center: 4.1X revenue growth, 52 implant consultations/month.
- **Practice management integration.** Dentrix, Eaglesoft, Open Dental -- closed-loop attribution.

### WEAKNESSES (Internal Negative)
- **Very new company.** ~2 years old vs. competitors with 10-25 year track records. Limited battle-testing.
- **Zero third-party reviews.** No Clutch, G2, Trustpilot, or BBB presence. All social proof is self-reported.
- **"250+ clients" claim is unverifiable.** No client list, no case study volume to support. Only 3 case studies published.
- **GoHighLevel dependency.** Their "AI" is largely GoHighLevel workflows, not truly proprietary technology. Any agency can buy GoHighLevel for $97-$297/month.
- **Unknown team size and composition.** No team page. Who does the work? How many people? What are their qualifications?
- **Dan Wang has no dental industry speaking presence.** No podcast, no conferences, no published content in dental journals.
- **Cosmetic-only niche limits TAM.** Cannot serve general dentistry, pediatric, or orthodontic practices.
- **Pay-per-appointment math can get expensive.** $450-$550 per All-on-4 appointment x 20 appointments/month = $9,000-$11,000/month, which is expensive for practices used to flat retainers.

- **$10M+ attributable revenue claim is vague.** Across all 250 clients over multiple years, this is modest ($40K per client lifetime).

- **First-mover in pay-per-appointment.** No other competitor has adopted this model at scale. If it works, it's defensible.
- **AI technology gap.** Most competitors have minimal AI. Cosmetics Growth could become the "AI-first dental marketing agency."

- **Copycats.** Pay-per-appointment is a model, not a moat. Any agency could adopt this pricing.
- **GoHighLevel saturation.** As more agencies use GoHighLevel, the "AI-powered" claim becomes meaningless.
- **Client math problems.** If appointment volume spikes, the per-appointment model becomes more expensive than a retainer, causing client complaints.
- **No industry credibility.** Without conference presence, publications, or industry relationships, Cosmetics Growth is invisible to the dental establishment.
- **Due diligence failures.** Sophisticated buyers who can't find team info, third-party reviews, or verifiable claims will walk away.
