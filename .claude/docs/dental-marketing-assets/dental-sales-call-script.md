# Dental Practice Sales Call Script
## For calls booked through creeksidemarketing.net

**[NOTE: Cade delivers this script. Adjust first-person references accordingly.]**

**Who uses this:** Cade, Creekside Marketing
**Call type:** Discovery/close hybrid (15-20 minutes)
**Lead source:** Dental landing page (application form or lead magnet download)
**Tone:** Peer-to-peer. Business owner to business owner. Direct, specific, no fluff.

---

## 1. PRE-CALL PREP (5 minutes before the call)

Open these before dialing:

**From their application form:**
- Practice name and location (Google it, look at their website and reviews)
- Monthly revenue they reported
- Current ad spend and who manages it
- Number of cosmetic and implant consultations per month
- What they said their biggest challenge is

**Quick research:**
- Google "[practice name] veneers" or "[practice name] cosmetic dentistry" to see if they have ads running
- Check their Google Business Profile (review count, rating, photos)
- Look at their website. Is it modern? Does it have before/after galleries? Is there a clear CTA?
- Check their Instagram/Facebook. How many followers? What kind of content? Any paid ads visible in the Ad Library?

**Have ready:**
- The Paid Ads Blueprint and industry benchmarks (cost per consultation, case acceptance, case value)
- A calculator or spreadsheet to do quick math on the call
- The Dr. Laleh case study numbers: 50-60 consults to 105+, $1M to $2M revenue, $200K+ additional monthly profit, same $110K ad spend, cost per lead dropped from $48.79 to $9.58, cost per consultation dropped from ~$2,200 to ~$1,050, ROAS from 2.7X to 5.7X

**Mental checklist:**
- What is their likely biggest lever? (You won't know for sure until discovery, but form a hypothesis from the application)
- Are they a real fit? ($10K+ monthly ad spend or willingness to get there, cosmetic and implant focus)
- What objections are most likely based on what they wrote?

**[Wait for confirmation before proceeding]**

## 3. DISCOVERY QUESTIONS (5-7 minutes)

Ask these in order. Take notes. The answers determine which lever to diagnose in the next section.

### Revenue and Scale

"What's your practice doing in monthly revenue right now?"
- (Confirm what they put on the application. If different, note it.)

"And roughly what percentage of that is cosmetic? Veneers, smile makeovers, implants vs. general dentistry?"

### Current Marketing

"What are you spending on ads right now per month, and on which platforms?"

"Who's managing that for you? In-house, agency, or are you doing it yourself?"

"How long have you been running ads?"

### Pipeline Numbers

"How many cosmetic consultations are you booking per month from your ads?"
- (This is the critical number. If they don't know, that's a red flag AND an opportunity.)

"What's your case acceptance rate on those consultations? What percentage of people who come in for a consult actually book a case?"
- (Most practices don't track this precisely. If they say "I'm not sure," say: "That's normal. Most practices don't track that number, and it's actually one of the biggest hidden levers.")

"What's your average case value for a cosmetic case? Are we talking mostly single veneers, or full smile makeovers?"

### Pain Point

"What would you say is your biggest bottleneck right now? Is it getting enough leads, converting consultations into cases, or something else entirely? Like leads that go nowhere, tire-kickers, low-quality leads?"

### History

"Have you worked with a marketing agency before? What happened?"
- (LISTEN. Do not defend agencies. If they got burned, this is your opening. If they're currently with one, find out what's not working.)

### Vision

"If we're having this same call 90 days from now and everything went perfectly, what does your practice look like? What changed?"
- (Get a specific number if you can. "I want 20 more consultations a month" is better than "I want to grow.")

---

## 4. DIAGNOSIS (3-4 minutes)

Based on their answers, identify which of the 3 profit levers is their biggest opportunity. Only focus on ONE. Trying to fix everything at once dilutes the pitch.

### Framework: The 3 Profit Levers

1. **Cost per consultation** (they're spending too much per booked consult)
2. **Case acceptance rate** (consults are coming in but not converting to cases)
3. **Case value** (they're getting single-veneer cases instead of full smile makeovers)

### How to Diagnose

**If their cost per consultation is above $1,500:**

"Here's what I'm seeing. You're spending [X] a month and getting [Y] consultations. That puts your cost per consultation at [X/Y]. The benchmark for an optimized cosmetic practice is $800 to $1,200. Top 5% are under $1,000.

That gap is costing you. At 20 consultations a month, the difference between $2,000 per consult and $1,000 per consult is $20,000 a month in wasted spend. And it's dragging your return on ad spend way below where it should be.

We had this exact situation with Dr. Laleh. She was spending $110,000 a month and stuck at 50-60 consultations. Her return on ad spend was 2.7X. Her agency was optimizing for leads, not consultations. We didn't increase her spend. We changed what we optimized for. Six months later: 105 consultations a month. 5.7X return on ad spend. Cost per consultation dropped by over 50%.

Based on what you're telling me, if we just brought your cost per consultation from [current] down to [target], that's [math] in savings per month. And your return on ad spend would jump significantly. That's before we even talk about increasing volume."

**If their case acceptance is below 35%:**

"So you're booking [X] consultations a month, and your acceptance rate is around [Y]%. Here's where the opportunity is.

Industry average is 25-35%. Optimized practices run 40-50%. If we move your acceptance rate from [current] to 40%, that's [math] additional cases per month at your average case value. That's an extra [dollar amount] in monthly revenue without spending a single extra dollar on ads.

We've seen this pattern before. Most of the time, the fix isn't more leads. It's what happens after the consultation. Financing options beyond just CareCredit, same-day treatment presentation, automated follow-up for the patients who say they need to think about it. Those patients aren't gone. They just need a system to bring them back."

**If their average case value is low (mostly single veneers):**

"Here's something most agencies completely miss. A single veneer case is $1,500 to $2,500 in revenue. A full smile makeover is $12,000 to $20,000. Full-arch implants run $25K-$50K per case. On a profit-per-hour-of-chair-time basis, full makeovers are 5 times more profitable.

If your ads are generating single-veneer inquiries instead of full makeover patients, you're filling your schedule with low-margin work. The fix is in the ad creative and targeting. The messaging needs to anchor on the total transformation, not the per-tooth price. Before-and-after imagery of complete makeovers, not individual teeth.

Based on your numbers, if we shifted even 30% of your consults from single-veneer to full smile makeover inquiries, that's an additional [math] in monthly profit."

### Always End the Diagnosis With a Specific Number

"Based on what you're telling me, if we just improved your [lever] from [current] to [target], that's an additional $[amount] per month in profit. Over a year, that's $[annual amount]."

## 5. THE OFFER (2-3 minutes)

**Transition:**

"So here's how we work. I'll keep it simple because it is simple."

### Pricing

"Our management fee is a percentage of your ad spend. It starts at 20% and comes down as your spend grows. At higher spend levels, the rate drops to 15%, then 10%. No cap on the upside.

At your spend level of [their reported spend], your management fee would be about [calculate]. That covers everything: ad management across Meta, Google, or both. Landing page builds. Creative production. Reporting. CRM consultation for your follow-up systems. One fee, full service."

### Ad Spend

"You pay your ad spend directly to Meta or Google. We don't mark it up."

### Contract Terms

"90-day initial term, then month-to-month after that. If we're not delivering, you leave. No buyout clauses, no runaround. I'd rather earn your business every month than rely on fine print to keep you."

### Guarantee

"If we don't hit 3X return on your ad spend within 90 days, we work for free until we do. Multi-touch attribution tied to actual revenue. That's not a marketing line. That's the agreement."

### Market Exclusivity

"One thing that's important. We won't work with a competing practice in your area. If we take you on, your market is yours. We're not running the same playbook for two practices in the same zip code."

### What They Get

"To break it down: full-service ad management, custom landing pages built specifically for cosmetic case generation, ad creative including graphics and video, monthly reporting on the metrics that actually matter, which is your return on ad spend and consultations booked, not just impressions and clicks. And CRM consultation so your follow-up system actually converts the leads we send you."

### Timeline

"Timeline is 30 days to launch. That's onboarding, account setup, creative production, landing page build, and campaign launch. Then 60 to 90 days to full optimization. Month 1 is learning and foundation. Month 2 is optimization and testing. Month 3 is when you start seeing the returns move in a meaningful way."

## 6. OBJECTION HANDLING

### "I already have an agency."

"Totally fair. Quick question. Do you know what your return on ad spend is right now? For every dollar you put into ads, how many dollars come back in revenue?

That's the number we build everything around. Not leads, not clicks, not impressions. Revenue back per dollar in.

When Dr. Laleh started working with us, her return on ad spend was 2.7X. Now it's 5.7X. That's the difference between $300,000 and $630,000 in revenue on the same ad budget. We also run ads across platforms most dental practices aren't even on yet, like ChatGPT Ads and programmatic.

If you ever want to compare approaches, grab the Paid Ads Blueprint. It'll show you exactly where your opportunities are across every platform."

### "That's too expensive."

"I get it. Let me put it in context. At your spend level, your management fee is [amount]. You need ONE additional $15,000 case to more than cover it.

The percentage model actually works in your favor. We're incentivized to make every dollar of your ad spend work harder. We don't make more by telling you to spend more for no reason. We make more when you grow because the results justify it.

Dr. Laleh is getting a 5.7X return on her ad spend. For every dollar she puts in, $5.70 comes back in revenue. The management fee is a fraction of that return."

### "I've been burned before."

"That's actually the norm. Most practices I talk to have been through one or two agencies before they find us.

I hear that from almost every practice I talk to. And honestly, that's exactly why we keep our terms straightforward. If we don't perform, we make it easy to part ways. No runaround, no buyout clauses.

We've kept Dr. Laleh as a client for almost a year now because the results speak for themselves. She went from a 2.7X to a 5.7X return on her ad spend. 50 consultations to over 105 a month. That's not a pitch. That's why she stays.

But I also understand trust is earned, not promised. So here's what I'd say. Give us 90 days. If by month 3 you're not seeing a meaningful improvement in your return on ad spend, you leave. Zero risk."

### "I need to think about it."

"Totally understand. What specifically do you need to think through? Is it the investment, the timing, or something about how we work?"

**(Then LISTEN. The answer tells you the real objection.)**

- If it's the money: go back to the ROI math.
- If it's timing: "When would be a better time? Keep in mind, every month you wait is another month of [wasted spend / missed consultations]."
- If they need to talk to a partner: "Makes sense. What would your partner need to see to feel good about this? I can put together a one-page summary with your specific numbers."
- If they're comparing agencies: "Smart. What are you comparing on? I'd rather know now so I can make sure you have what you need to make the right call."

### "Can you do it for less?"

"The percentage covers everything. Ads, landing pages, creative, reporting. Most agencies charge separately for each of those line items. When you add it all up, you're usually paying more with them than you would with us. And our rate drops as you scale, so the more successful you are, the lower your effective rate goes.

I'd rather compete on returns than on price. The practices that work with us aren't looking for the cheapest option. They're looking for the one that actually delivers the highest return on every dollar they spend on ads."

### "What if it doesn't work?"

"If you're not seeing results by month 3, we make it simple to walk. No runaround. But in our experience, practices see consultation quality improve within the first 30 days.

But let me tell you what we typically see. Within the first 30 days, consultation quality improves because we're filtering for higher-intent patients. By month 2, we've A/B tested enough to know what creative and targeting is working. By month 3, the system is optimized and you're seeing a measurable increase in booked consultations and return on ad spend.

The 90-day investment framework isn't us buying time. It's how paid ads actually work. Month 1 is data. Month 2 is optimization. Month 3 is performance."

### "I don't have the ad budget."

"What's the minimum you could start with? We recommend $5,000 a month in ad spend as a starting point. That gives us enough data to prove the model and optimize effectively.

At $5,000 in ad spend, your management fee at 20% is $1,000. So all-in, you're at $6,000 a month. Based on the benchmarks we've seen, you should be generating a 3 to 5X return on that total investment within 90 days. That's $18,000 to $30,000 in revenue from a $6,000 total investment.

And honestly, if you can't invest $6,000 a month in growing your cosmetic cases, we might not be the right fit right now. I'd rather tell you that upfront than take your money when the budget is too tight to get real results."

### "How is this different from [competitor agency]?"

"Two things. First, we specialize exclusively in paid ads for dental practices. New patients, implants, cosmetic cases, smile makeovers. Google, Meta, TikTok, programmatic, ChatGPT Ads, Bing. We don't do SEO, organic social, or content marketing. Paid patient acquisition is all we do, and we run it across every platform that generates cases for dental practices, including channels like ChatGPT Ads that most people haven't even heard of yet.

Second, we optimize for return on ad spend, not leads generated. Leads are a vanity metric. A lead that never sits in your chair costs you money. Dr. Laleh's return went from 2.7X to 5.7X. For every dollar she puts into ads, she gets $5.70 back in revenue. That's a fundamentally different optimization target, and it produces fundamentally different results."

## MINDSET REMINDERS

- You're evaluating them as much as they're evaluating you. Not every practice is a fit. Say no when it's not.
- You are a business owner talking to a business owner. Not a salesperson pitching a service.
- If they mention a service you don't do (SEO, social media management, email marketing), say so. "We don't do that. We specialize in paid patient acquisition for cosmetic cases. That's it. Jack of all trades, master of none."
- Never discount. Never negotiate on price. Compete on quality and results.
- After presenting pricing, stop talking. Silence is your closer.
- The goal is not to convince them. The goal is to show them the math and let the math do the convincing.
